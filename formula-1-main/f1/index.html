Lewis Hamilton

Lewis Carl Davidson Hamilton was born on 7 January 1985 in Stevenage, Hertfordshire, England. It was clear from very early on that he had a natural talent for racing. In 1998, Ron Dennis signed him to the McLaren's Young Driver Programme. He broke down racial barriers as a junior kart racer and he remains the sport's only black driver. One thing he always remember is what his father Anthony told him: “Always do your talking on the track". And he has done that. He's one of the greatest drivers of F1 history, holding several records.

Charles Leclerc

Charles Marc Hervé Perceval Leclerc was born on 16 October 1997 in Monte Carlo, Monaco. After his success in GP3 and F2 he was an obvious choice for an F1 seat. He's referred as Ferrari's golden boy since joining the team in 2019 and he put pressure on the 4-time world champion Sebastian Vettel. He has what it takes to be the next F1 world champion. He's a gifted young driver and as his former teammate Vettel said "he is something special".

Lando Norris

Lando Norris was born in 13 November 1999 in Glastonbury, England. Before landing a Fl seat he was tipped as Fl's next Lewis Hamilton. After having a successful racing career across karting, F4, F3, F2, and F1, he's one of the most promising and popular young drivers on the grid. He has become a vocal advocate for mental health awareness in recent years.

Max Verstappen

Max Emilian Verstappen was born on 30 September 1997 in Hasselt, Belgium and he's the son of former F1 driver Jos Verstappen. But he's so much more. He was born to be fast. He impressed everyone in the early stages of his career and he chose to part of the Red Bull family in 2014. He's now a two-time world champion and he already set his mark on the sport and broke several F1 records. He's here to stay, to fight, to win and to be the greatest.

George Russell

George William Russell was born on 15 February 1998 in King's Lynn, Norfolk, England and he's considered one of the most talented young drivers in F1. He joined the Mercedes Junior Driver Programme in 2017 and in that year, he became the GP3 Series champion, winning F2 championship title in the year following. He was given a Fl seat in 2019 in one of the slowest cars of the field. But he impressed everyone with his ability to extract the most performance while outqualifying his teammates.

Carlos Sainz

Carlos Sainz Vázquez de Castro was born on 1 September 1994 in Madrid, Spain. Racing is in his blood since he's the son of Carlos Sainz Sr, a double World Rally Champion. Since his debut in 2015 he already drove for four different teams and he has a natural habit of adapting quickly to new teams and cars.

Fernando Alonso

Fernando Alonso Díaz was born on 29 July 1981 in Oviedo, Asturias, Spain. He was born to race but he didn't come from a wealthy family.He has to thank his older sister for his passion for racing since he was the one playing with the kart that was built for her. He showed off his talent in the junior categories and in 2001 a new star arrived at F1. Since then, he has proven his natural speed. In the end of 2018, he left F1 but it was never a "goodbye," just a "see you later",. ', since he came back in 2021 proving that age is not a decisive factor in F1.

Daniel Ricciardo

Daniel Ricciardo, the charismatic Australian racing driver, rose from karting prodigy to Formula 1 star. After successful years with Red Bull Racing, he joined McLaren in 2021, but left in 2022 seeking new challenges. In the 2023 season, he served as Red Bull's third driver before replacing Nyck De Vries at Alpha Tauri for the Hungarian Grand Prix. With his infectious smile and tenacity on the track, Ricciardo continues to be a fan favorite. Now focused on podium finishes, he aims to elevate Alpha Tauri's position in the midfield battle, leaving a lasting mark on the world of motorsport.
